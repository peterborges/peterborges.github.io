H-E-B Assortment Tool

Smarter assortment planning at scale

Timeline: Jan 2019 – Mar 2021

Roles: Strategy, Competitive Research, Experience Design, User Research, Site Analytics

Team: 1 PO · 1 Researcher · 2 Designers · 1 Scrum Master · 8 Developers

Overview

H-E-B is a leading Texas-based supermarket chain with over 350 locations. The Assortment Tool project aimed to give buyers and analysts better insight into the cost, value, and impact of shelf decisions—moving from gut feel to data-driven actions.

Outcomes

  • $39M in reduced shelf planning costs (1-year cycle reduced to 3 months)
  • 96% reduction in labor costs for tailoring (20 hours down to 1 hour per activity)
  • Increased active users from 3 to over 60 monthly

Challenges

Buyers struggled to understand the ROI of their decisions due to fragmented systems and inaccessible data. Assortment updates often led to costly missteps or poor store alignment.

Design Goals

  • Increase speed to shelf
  • Standardize processes across stores and categories
  • Improve profitability and strategic innovation

Approach

Story Mapping & Research

We aligned stakeholders through story mapping and regular user charrettes. These research sprints built empathy and drove faster iteration across the complex ecosystem of H-E-B’s 400+ internal tools.

Understanding Users

We designed for two core user types:

  • Business Development Managers (BDMs): Balance product mix and profitability across categories
  • Shelf Edge Analysts (SEAs): Tailor assortments for store performance and changing demand

Design System & Information Architecture

We created an IA that worked within the broader suite of H-E-B tools (like Product Attribute Manager and Visual Planner) while introducing modular flows like:

  • Decision Tree: Evaluate item mix, demand, and strategy
  • New Item Queue: Prioritize and compare new vs existing items
  • Assortment Optimizer: Model and finalize item selection
  • Shelf Edge Calendar: View and manage all planning activities

Analytics & Reporting

Users gained new visibility into assortment health, out-of-stocks, and ROI. Visual dashboards empowered better strategic planning and decision validation.

Reflections

This project transformed how a key business function operated at H-E-B. By designing in sync with existing workflows and elevating user research, we delivered massive ROI and long-term usability improvements.
